# Organization presentation: IT Company Providing Equipment Sales, System Integration, Security Integration, and Support Services

## 1. Company Overview

**Company name:** SecureNet d.o.o.  
**Industry:** Information technology services and system integration  
**Company size:** 45 employees  
**Location:** One main office, with hybrid work and on-site client visits  
**Business type:** Small to medium-sized enterprise (SME)

SecureNet d.o.o. is an IT company that specializes in:

- sales of IT and network equipment,
- system integration,
- integration of cybersecurity solutions,
- IT support and maintenance services for business clients.

The company primarily serves other organizations in the B2B market, including small and medium-sized businesses. Some clients operate in sectors with elevated security and compliance requirements, such as manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, and professional services.

Because the company supports and maintains client IT environments, it is responsible not only for protecting its own information systems, but also for safeguarding sensitive technical data, administrative access, service availability, and client trust.

---

## 2. Organizational Structure

The company has 45 employees divided into the following functional groups:

- **Management and administration:** 5
- **Sales and account management:** 8
- **System engineers / integrators:** 12
- **Security engineers:** 6
- **Service desk / support staff:** 8
- **Procurement and logistics:** 3
- **Finance and HR:** 3

### Key operational characteristics

- A significant part of the workforce operates in a hybrid model.
- Engineers frequently access client systems remotely.
- The company stores sensitive technical documentation related to client environments.
- A large share of revenue comes from maintenance and support contracts governed by SLAs.
- Downtime in internal systems directly affects service delivery, revenue, and reputation.

## 3. Business Model and Core Processes

### Main business activities

#### 3.1 Equipment Sales

The company sells:

- servers,
- workstations,
- network devices,
- firewalls,
- endpoint protection solutions,
- Microsoft 365 and other software licenses.

#### 3.2 System Integration

The company designs, deploys, and configures:

- server environments,
- virtualization platforms,
- business networks,
- VPN solutions,
- backup systems,
- identity and access management solutions.

#### 3.3 Security Solution Integration

The company implements:

- firewalls,
- EDR/XDR solutions,
- MFA,
- SIEM integrations,
- network segmentation,
- baseline security policies and governance controls.

#### 3.4 Support and Maintenance

The company provides:

- helpdesk support,
- remote administration,
- patch management,
- incident handling,
- troubleshooting,
- regular maintenance interventions at client sites.

### Critical business processes

The most important business processes are:

- processing sales orders and quotations,
- planning and executing integration projects,
- delivering remote support to clients,
- managing technical documentation and configurations,
- invoicing and contract administration,
- communication with clients and suppliers.

## 4. IT Environment

### 4.1 Infrastructure

The company uses a hybrid environment combining cloud services and on-premises infrastructure.

#### Main systems

- **Microsoft 365** for email, Teams, SharePoint, and OneDrive
- **ERP system** for quotations, orders, invoicing, and inventory
- **CRM system** for customer relationship management
- **PSA / ticketing system** for support requests and service operations
- **Remote Monitoring and Management (RMM)** platform for managing client devices
- **Document repository** for project files, configurations, and client documentation
- **Password vault / privileged access storage**
- **NAS / backup server** for internal backups
- **VPN solution** for remote access by employees
- **Virtualization environment** for internal services and test systems

### 4.2 Endpoints

The organization uses:

- 35 company laptops,
- 5 desktop computers,
- 10 corporate smartphones,
- several test and lab devices,
- 2–3 internal servers or virtualization hosts,
- office networking equipment.

### 4.3 Network

The office environment includes:

- a corporate LAN,
- employee Wi-Fi,
- guest Wi-Fi,
- VPN access for remote users,
- administrative and support connections to client environments through VPN, RMM, and secure portals.
